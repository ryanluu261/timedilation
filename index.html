Time Dilation

Explanation

This is a website portraying the concept of time dilation, a direct result of Special Relativity. (I tried to build a light cone calculator but the Minkowski equation was a lot harder to integrate with graphics than this formula of time dilation that I found below, so I went with this.) In essence, time dilation suggests that as you approach the speed of light, time appears to slow down for you. For instance, if you were traveling at 50% of the speed of light away from Earth, an observer remaining stationary on Earth would perceive your clock as ticking slower. From their perspective, 1 second for you would appear to be 1.15 seconds.

In the equation above: t is the time seen by a stationary observer, t0 is the time in a rest frame, v is your velocity and c is the speed of light (186282 miles/second)
